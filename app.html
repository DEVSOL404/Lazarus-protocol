What counts as you being alive

A sleeping wallet can still receive money, get airdrops, and execute pre-signed transactions. None of those prove you're alive. Only deliberate, signed-now activity does.

✓
Outgoing transactions you sign
Sending ETH, swapping tokens, calling any contract method. Anything that requires your signature in real time.
✓
Direct heartbeat clicks
"Record activity" on the dashboard signs a one-line tx that just says "I'm here." Costs only gas.
✓
Lazarus actions
Granting a role, setting a Silence Window, configuring your baseline, accepting an invite. Every signed Lazarus interaction is a heartbeat.
✓
Mobile companion pulse (coming)
Biometric-attested check-in from the Lazarus mobile app. Doesn't cost gas.
×
Receiving funds
Anyone can send ETH or tokens to your address. That's their action, not yours. Doesn't count.
×
Pre-signed or scheduled transactions
A timelock unlock or a pre-signed swap broadcast by someone else doesn't prove you're alive. You signed it weeks ago.
×
Airdrops, MEV, automated callbacks
Bots claiming airdrops on your behalf, contract-to-contract callbacks, distributor sweeps. None of these involve you signing anything.
×
Reverted transactions
A failed tx still has your signature, but if it didn't execute, it's not counted as a confirmed action.
